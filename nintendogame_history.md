# Nintendo Game History: Famous Games Year by Year

## 1889-1970s: The Early Days

### 1889
- **Nintendo founded** — Fusajiro Yamauchi began manufacturing "Hanafuda" playing cards in Kyoto, Japan

### 1963
- Company name changed to **Nintendo Co., Ltd.**, beginning manufacturing of games alongside playing cards

### 1977
- **Color TV-Game 6** — First home console released in Japan (June 1, 1977)
- **Color TV-Game 15** — Released June 8, 1977
- **Color TV-Racing 112** — Released June 8, 1978
- **Color TV-Block Kuzushi** — Released April 23, 1979

### 1978
- **EVR Race** — Nintendo's first electromechanical arcade game
- **Sheriff** — Arcade game

### 1979
- **Space Launcher** — Arcade game

---

## 1980s: Arcade Era & Game & Watch

### 1980
- **Game & Watch Ball** — First Game & Watch handheld (April 28, 1980)
- **Game & Watch Flagman** — Second Game & Watch title
- **Game & Watch Vermin**
- **Game & Watch Fire**
- **Game & Watch Judge**
- **Space Fever** — Arcade game

### 1981
- **Donkey Kong** — Arcade game designed by Shigeru Miyamoto (July 9, 1981)
  - Nintendo's first major international hit
  - Introduced Mario (originally "Jumpman") to the world
  - One of the most influential platform games ever made

### 1982
- **Donkey Kong Jr.** — Arcade (1982)
- **Sky Skipper** — Arcade

### 1983
- **Donkey Kong 3** — Arcade
- **Mario Bros.** — Arcade (March 14, 1983)
- **Popeye** — Arcade
- **Famicom (Family Computer)** launched in Japan (July 15, 1983)
- **Computer Othello** — Arcade

### 1984
- **Excitebike** — NES
- **Punch-Out!!** — Arcade / NES
- **VS. Balloon Fight** — Arcade
- **VS. Baseball** — Arcade
- **VS. Duck Hunt** — Arcade
- **Stroke & Match Golf** — NES

### 1985: The NES Revolution
- **NES (Nintendo Entertainment System)** launched in North America (October 18, 1985)
- **Super Mario Bros.** — NES (October 18, 1985)
  - Revolutionized the platformer genre
  - Introduced side-scrolling gameplay
  - One of the best-selling games of all time
- **Duck Hunt** — NES
- **Gyromite** — NES
- **Stack-Up** — NES
- **Ice Climber** — NES
- **Wrecking Crew** — NES

### 1986
- **The Legend of Zelda** — Famicom/NES (February 21, 1986)
  - Introduced Link and the kingdom of Hyrule
  - Pioneered save game functionality with battery backup
  - Founded one of gaming's most beloved franchises
- **Kid Icarus** — NES
- **Metroid** — NES (August 6, 1986)
  - Introduced Samus Aran
  - Pioneered exploration-based platforming
- **The Legend of Zelda II: The Adventure of Link** — NES

### 1987
- **Super Mario Bros. 2** — NES (October 9, 1987)
- **Metroid** — Famicom Disk System (1987)
- **Fire Emblem: Ankido Ryu** — Famicom (January 20, 1987)
  - First Fire Emblem game
  - Established the tactical RPG genre
- **Star Fy** — Arcade

### 1988
- **Super Mario Bros. 3** — NES (October 23, 1988)
  - Introduced world maps and power-ups
  - Considered one of the greatest NES games
- **The Legend of Zelda: The Adventure of Link** — NES (September 26, 1988)
- **EarthBound Zero** — NES (1988)
- **Punch-Out!!** featuring Mr. Dream — NES

### 1989: Game Boy Arrives
- **Game Boy** launched (April 21, 1989 in Japan)
- **Tetris** — Game Boy (bundled with the system in North America)
  - Best-selling Game Boy game of all time
  - Global phenomenon
- **Super Mario Land** — Game Boy (April 21, 1989)
  - First portable Mario game
- **Baseball** — Game Boy
- **Nintendo World Championships 1990** — NES
- **Super Mario Bros. 3** — NES (1988 in Japan, 1989 worldwide)

---

## 1990s: Super Nintendo Era

### 1990
- **Super Famicom (Super Nintendo Entertainment System)** launched in Japan (November 21, 1990)
- **Super Mario World** — SNES (December 21, 1990 in Japan)
  - Launch title for SNES
  - Introduced Yoshi
- **F-Zero** — SNES (1990)
  - Pioneered Mode 7 racing graphics

### 1991
- **Super NES** launched in North America (August 23, 1991)
- **The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past** — SNES (December 13, 1991 in Japan)
  - Considered one of the greatest games of all time
- **Super Mario World** — SNES North America (1991)
- **Pilotwings** — SNES

### 1992
- **Kirby's Dream Land** — Game Boy (April 27, 1992)
  - Introduced Kirby, the "pink puff"
- **Super Mario Kart** — SNES (August 27, 1992)
  - First Mario Kart game
  - Pioneered kart racing genre
- **The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past** — SNES North America (1992)

### 1993
- **Star Fox** — SNES (February 23, 1993)
  - Introduced 3D polygon graphics
  - First appearance of Fox McCloud
- **Donkey Kong Country** — SNES (November 18, 1994 in Japan, 1994 worldwide)
  - Stunning pre-rendered 3D graphics
  - Revitalized Donkey Kong franchise
- **Kirby's Dream Land 2** — Game Boy

### 1994
- **Super Metroid** — SNES (March 19, 1994)
  - Considered one of the greatest games ever made
  - Masterpiece of atmosphere and design
- **Donkey Kong Country** — SNES (November 18, 1994)
- **Yoshi's Island (Yoshi's Island: Super Mario Advance 3)** — SNES (October 4, 1995 in Japan)
- **EarthBound** — SNES (June 27, 1994 in Japan)
- **Kirby's Dream Land 3** — SNES

### 1995
- **Chrono Trigger** — SNES (March 11, 1995)
- **Donkey Kong Country 2: Diddy's Kong Quest** — SNES
- **Super Mario World 2: Yoshi's Island** — SNES (October 4, 1995)
- **The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening** — Game Boy (1993 in Japan, 1995 worldwide)
- **Mario Kart 64** — Nintendo 64 (1996 in Japan)

### 1996: Nintendo 64 Era Begins
- **Nintendo 64** launched in Japan (June 23, 1996)
- **Super Mario 64** — N64 (June 23, 1996)
  - Pioneered 3D platforming
  - First 3D Mario game
- **Pilotwings 64** — N64 (launch title in Japan)
- **The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time** — N64 (November 21, 1998)
  - Revolutionized 3D action-adventure games
  - Considered one of the greatest games ever made
- **Pokémon Red and Green** — Game Boy (February 27, 1996 in Japan)
  - Created the Pokémon phenomenon

### 1997
- **Pokémon Red and Blue** — Game Boy (1997 in North America)
- **GoldenEye 007** — N64 (August 25, 1997)
  - Pioneered first-person shooter on consoles
  - Multiplayer landmark
- **Diddy Kong Racing** — N64
- **Star Fox 64** — N64 (April 25, 1997)

### 1998
- **The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time** — N64 (November 21, 1998)
- **Pokémon Yellow** — Game Boy (1998 in Japan)
- **Mario Kart 64** — N64 (1996 in Japan, 1997 in North America)
- **Banjo-Kazooie** — N64 (1998)
- **1080° Snowboarding** — N64 (1998)
- **F-Zero X** — N64 (1998)

### 1999
- **The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask** — N64 (April 27, 2000 in Japan)
- **Super Smash Bros.** — N64 (January 21, 1999 in Japan)
  - Crossover fighting game
  - Huge success
- **Donkey Kong 64** — N64
- **Mario Party** — N64
- **Pokémon Stadium** — N64

## Historical Milestones

- **1889**: Nintendo founded as a playing card company
- **1981**: Donkey Kong introduces Mario
- **1985**: NES revitalizes the video game industry
- **1986**: The Legend of Zelda and Metroid launch
- **1989**: Game Boy launches with Tetris
- **1991**: Super NES brings 16-bit era
- **1996**: Nintendo 64 pioneers 3D gaming
- **1996**: Pokémon launches in Japan
- **2001**: GameCube and Game Boy Advance
- **2004**: Nintendo DS dual-screen innovation
- **2006**: Wii motion controls change gaming
- **2011**: Nintendo 3DS brings stereoscopic 3D
- **2017**: Nintendo Switch hybrid console
- **2025**: Nintendo Switch 2 launches

Nintendo has created some of the most iconic and beloved video game franchises in history, spanning over four decades of innovation and entertainment. From the original Mario in Donkey Kong to the modern open-world adventures of The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom, Nintendo continues to shape the gaming industry with creative gameplay, memorable characters, and groundbreaking hardware.
